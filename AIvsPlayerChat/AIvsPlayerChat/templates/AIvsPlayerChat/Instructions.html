In each round, the bot will select one proposal from two proposals, namely, Proposal 1 and Proposal 2, on dividing up ten dollars between Player A and Player B.
When the computer proposes Y dollars to Player A and 10 – Y dollars to Player B,
if the Player B accepts the proposal,
then the Player A will receive Y dollars and Player B will receive 10 – Y dollars;
if Player B rejects the proposal, then both Player A and B will receive 0 dollars.

The bot will choose Proposal 1 with probability P and Proposal 2 with probability 1–P.
The probability P is determined based on the actual decisions made by participants in a similar study.
In a recent study conducted among NTU students, proposal was chosen by Player A him/herself instead of the bot.
In that study, P proportion of Player A chose Proposal 1 and 1-P proportion chose Proposal 2.


Please wait for all the players to arrive.
